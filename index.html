My raft on the river

By, Mara Bautista

My life's a river, wild and free,
Sometimes a torrent, crashing over me,
Sometimes a whisper, soft and low,
Secrets it holds, only I can know.

A winding path, I couldn't foresee,
Through sun-drenched fields, where I long to be, And shadowed canyons, deep and vast, Where echoes of heartbreak forever cast.

I built my raft, with trembling hands,
From splintered dreams and hopeful strands,
The current pulls, I fight and strive,
Through joy and sorrow, I somehow survive.

The rapids churn, a fearsome fight, Testing my strength, both day and night, But in the stillness, waters clear, My reflection shines, conquering fear.

I've met companions, along the way,
Some shared a moment, some stayed to stay, T
heir hands in mine, we bravely faced, The changing tides, our spirits embraced.

And as my river nears its end, The ocean waits, a journey to transcend,
I'll leave behind the shores I knew,
Embrace the mystery, forever true. For me.
